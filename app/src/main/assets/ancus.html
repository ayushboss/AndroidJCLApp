Ancus Marcius (640 BC – 616 BC)


Ancus Marcius was a Sabine man, like Numa Pompilius.

Ancus Marcius was the son of Pompilia, daughter of Numa Pompilius. Ancus Marcius was therefore Numa Pompilius’ grandson.

Built the Pons Sublicius.

Built the Tullianum, the infamous dungeon of the Mamertine.

Founded the port city of Ostia, and constructed salt works there.

Created the Plebeian class.

Added the Janiculum Hill to Rome.

Adopted the ius fetiale, a legal procedure for declaring war, from the tribe of the Aequicolae. Fetial priestsThrowing spear into enemy territory after 33 days of discussion, etc.

Built the Quirites’ trench, a trench on the other side of the city.

Added the Maesian Forest to Rome, which extended Roman holdings to the Mediterranean sea.
