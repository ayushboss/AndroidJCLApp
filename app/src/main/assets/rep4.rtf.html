The Second (Great) Samnite War (327 – 304 BC: 327 – 301, 316 – 304 BC)


  1. The Samnites seized Neapolis (Naples), and Neapolis’ citizens asked Rome for help. Rome came to help Neapolis. 
  2. The Battle of the Caudine Forks (321 BC): The Samnite commander Gavius Pontius defeats the Roman consuls Spurius Postumius Albinus and Veturius Calvinus and makes them go oundel the yoke. Gavius Pontius was advised by his father Herennius. The dictator Lucius Papirius Cursor takes command after Caudine Forks.
    1. Papirius Cursor was dictator twice, censor once, and consul five times. Fabius Rullianus is later also consul five times.
  3. The Battle of Lautulae (315 BC): Fabius Rullianus is trapped in a low mountain pass and Rome suffers a second major disaster.  
  4. The Battle of Lake Vadimo (310 BC): The dictator Papirius Cursor and the consul Fabius Rullianus defeat the Etruscan League (Samnite Allies).
  5. The Battle of Bovianum (305 BC): The Romans end the Second Samnite War.
