Antony and Octavian


  1. Marc Antony was Caesar’s relative and his consular colleague in 44 BC. Antony quickly persuaded Calpurnia, Caesar’s widow, to hand over all of his papers.
  2. Lepidus was preparing to besiege the conspirators on the Capitol with a newly recruited legion, but Antony persuaded Lepidus to refrain. Antony called a meeting of the seante on March 17th, where he was cool and conciliatory.
  3. On March 20, Antony delivered the funeral oration at Caesar’s oration. He displayed Caesar’s bloodied toga and a wax image of the corpse, leading to a riot and the cremation of Caesar’s corpse by burning down the senate house. 
    1. The main beneficiary was Gaius Octavius Thurinus, Caesar’s grandnephew, who was born at Velitrae to rich bankers and became now Caesar’s son by adoption.
    2. Antony profited from Caesar’s private funds, papers, and rough drafts. He made Lepidus the high pontiff. 
    3. Decimus Brutus fled to Cisalpine Gaul, and Trebonius fled to Asia. Brutus and Cassius lingered in Latium to find supporters. 
  4. Octavian
    1. Caesar had sent Octavius to Epirus for military training in preparation for the Parthian war. 
    2. Octavius took the adopted name Gaius Julius Caesar Octavianus. He always called himself Caesar. 
  5. Antony obtained a law giving him command of Cisalpine and Transalpine Gaul for five year and transferring Caesar’s legions there from Macedonia. 
    1. Brutus and Cassius demanded more signicant provinces thatn Crete and Cyrene. Antony got pissed and Brutus and Cassius went to find support in the East.
  6. Antony publicly criticized Cicero’s lack of attendance in the senate, leading to a war of words between Cicero and Antony including the fourteen Philippics (see lit outline). 
    1. Decimus Brutus was refusing to hand over his province of Cisalpine Gaul at the end of that year. Antony went to Brundisium, got his legions from Macedonia and prepared to drive Decimus Brutus out of Cisalpine Gaul. 
  7. The Battle of Mutina (44 – 43 BC): Antony trapped Decimus Brutus in Mutina. The senate sent Decimus Brutus reinforcements under the new consuls Hirtius and Pansa. Cicero demanded that the senate also send Octavian.
    1. Antony abandoned his siege of Mutina but retreated across the Alps into southern Gaul to get the support of Lepidus and Plancus.
    2. Hirtius and Pansa both died at Mutina. 
  8. The senate conferred suprerior command (imperium maius) over all Roman magistrates in the East on Brutus and Cassius, and voted Decimus Brutus a triumph, but only gave Octavian a minor triumph (ovatio). 
  9. Octavian marched on Rome and declared himself suffect consul with Quintus Pedius.
    1. Meanwhile Antony went to Lepidus’ camp and stole into it, thickly bearded, getting the men to defect to him. He returned to Cisalpine Gaul and Decimus Brutus’ army deserted. 


The Second Triumvirate 


  1. Octavian, Antony and Lepidus met near Bologna and formed their triumvirate (tresviri rei publicae constituendae) with absolute powers for five years. 
  2. The Lex Titia legalized the second triumvirate in 43 BC. 
    1. Octavian’s married Claudia to strengthen the Triumvirate.
    2. Antony got Cisalpine Gaul and Gallia Comata.
    3. Lepidus got Transalpine Gaul and the two Spains.
    4. Octavian got North Africa and Sicily, Sardinia and Corsica.
  3. Excursus: The Wives of Augustus

  4. The Proscriptions of 43 BC
    1. 130 senators and 2000 equites were among the victims.
    2. Cicero was killed on December 7, 43 BC, after abandoning his flight. Antony had insisted that he die. Fulvia stabbed his tongue in the Forum.
  5. Octavian started calling him Divi Filius and had a temple to Caesar dedicated in the Forum, and had him deified.
  6. The Battle of Philippi (42 BC): Brutus and Cassius took up positions on the Via Egnatia in Macedonia. Brutus defeated Octavian but Antony defeated Cassius (who committed suicide, unaware of Brutus’ victory). So Octavian and Antony won.
  7. Antony then went to the East and started doing his thing with Cleopatra.
  8. The Perusine War (40 BC): Antony’s wife Fulvia and his brother Lucius Antonius, a consul, tried to stir up the supsicon and hatred of landowners and veterans against Octavian, without Antony’s knowledge.
    1. Octavian, Agrippa, and Quintus Salvidienus maneuvered Lucius Antony and Fulvia into the Etruscan hillside town of Perusia and put them under siege. 
    2. Octavian executed Persusia’s town council but spared Lucius and Fulvia.
  9. Octavian divorced Claudia to marry his second wife, Scribonia, a much older woman.
    1. Scribonia was the aunt of Sextus Pompey, whom Octavian was making overtures to. Octavian did not know that Sextus Pompey had been making overtures to Antony.
  10. Quintus Labienus, son of Titus Labienus, was helping out the Parthians.
  11. Fulvia rejoined with Antony and urged him to ally with Sextus Pompey. She died shortly thereafter. Sextus Pompey and Antony allied.
  12. Octavian closed off the port of Brundisium to Antony, so Antony besieged Brundisium. His ally Sextus Pompey simultaneously struck against southern Italy.
  13. The Pact of Brundisium (40 BC): The Second Triumvirate was renewed.
  14. The Treaty of Misenum (39 BC): Sextus Pompey met with the triumvirate and agreed to stop blockading Italy and committing piracy, and to supply Rome with grain. The triumvirs met and banqueted with Sextus Pompey, agreeing to let him have Sicily and Sardinia, and to give him Corscia and the Pelopponesus, as well as compensation for his father’s confiscated lands and a future augurate and consulship. 


Antony, Livia, Pompey


  1. Antony set out for Athens with his bride Octavia (sister of Octavian). He sent Asinius Pollio to subdue the Parthni in the Balkans, and Ventidius Bassu and Herod to drive out the Parthian invaders from Syria, Palestine, and Asia Minor.
    1. Ventidius Bassus rolled back the Parthians to the Euphrates.
    2. Antonius sent Canidius to pacify Armenia, to avenge Carrhae. 
  2. Sextus Pompey got pissed at Antony’s delay in handing over the Pelopponesus and started blockading Italy again in 39 BC. Also, Octavian divorced his aunt Scribonia.
  3. Octavian married Livia Drusilla, the daughter of Livius Drusus Claudianus and the wife of Tiberius Claudius Nero (who had fought against Octavian at Perusia).
    1. Tiberius Claudius Nero agreed to divorce Livia for the good of the family.
    2. Livia’s son was Tiberius, and she was pregnant with Drusus I. 
    3. Octavian divorced Scribonia on the same day she bore him his only child, Julia. 
  4. Sextus Pompey (38 to 36 BC)
    1. When Octavian tried to invade Sicily, Sextus Pompey destroyed two of his fleets. Octavian had just gotten the traitorous governor of Sardinia to defect from Pompey.
    2. Octavian revoked Agrippa from Gaul and called on Antony’s help. 
  5. The Treaty of Tarentum (37 BC): Antony and Octavian renew the Second Triumvirate for the second time. It is to last another five years.
  6. The Battle of Naulochus (36 BC): Octavian’s admiral Agrippa crushed Sextus Pompey at a great naval battle near the Straits of Messana, though Octavian himself who had been defeated. 
    1. Lepidus insisted on accepting the surrender of Sicily in person. Octavian objected, so Lepidus ordered him off the island. Then Octavian snuck into Lepidus’ camp and used the name of Caesar to persuade his legions to desert. 
    2. When Octavian returned he was celebrated, and received the sacrosanctity of a plebeian tribune and the title Imperator Caesar. 
    3. Sextus Pompey was not captured and killed until he attacked Asia Minor in 35 BC.


Antony in the East


  1. Antony returned to the East in 37 BC, where he held a public ceremony with Cleopatra at Antioch. He did not divorce Octavia, who was politically valuable.
  2. Antony was associated with Dionysus, and Cleopatra with Aphrodite. To the Egyptians thery appeared as Osiris and Iris. Their children became Alexander Helios and Cleopatra Selene. In 36 BC, Cleopatra bore Antony a child called Ptolemy Philadelphus.
  3. Antony gave part of Syria and Cyrus to Cleopatra. Antony rejected her demand for the kingdom of Herod I of Judaea. But he did give Cleopatra Herod’s balsalm gardens at Jericho. 
  4. Antony prepared to invade Parthia, against Cleopatra’s advice. The expedition failed because Antony’s ally King Artavasdes of Armenia pulled out, losing Antony 20,000 men.


Civil War: Antony and Octavian


  1. Octavia was still loyal to Antony and brought him 2000 fresh troops in 35. At Athens, she received a message from Antony ordering her to send on the troops but go home herself. Antony finally divorced her, losing him support in Italy.
  2. Meanwhile, Antony invaded Armenia and carried off King Artavasdes to Egypt.
  3. Antony gave Cleopatra and her children additional territories and recognized her as the supreme overlord of all eastern client kingdoms in a ceremony called the Donations of Alexandria. 
    1. Antony and Cleopatra wintered at Ephesus, preparing for war with Octavian. 
  4. An adherent of Antony brought Octavian his will, which Antony had deposited with the Vestal Virgins. Octavian extorted the will and read it at a meeting of the senate. It confirmed the legacies to Cleopatra’s children as well as to Caesarion (whom it declared a true son and successor of Julius Caesar). It also said Antony was to be buried by Cleopatra in the Ptolemaic Museum in Alexandria. Romans were convinced Antony had been totally corrupted.
    1. Octavian got Antony stripped of his consulship for the upcoming year of 31 BC. 
    2. Octavian declared war on Cleopatra. 
  5. The Battle of Actium (31 BC): Agrippa set up a blockade that caused famine and plague in Antony’s camb during the summer of 31. Antony’s troops were paralyzed by desertion. Agrippa defeated Antony and Cleopatra for Octavian.
    1. Cleopatra got away with 60 ships, and Antony escaped as well. 
    2. Antony and Cleopatra committed suicide. Cleoptara’s death signalled the end of the Ptolemies that had ruled Egypt for 300 years
