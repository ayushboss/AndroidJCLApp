Septimius Severus

 

1.         1)      Septimius Severus had been born at Lepcis Magna in Africa Proconsularis. He had been governor of Gallia Lugdunensis and then Pannonia.

a. a.    Severus spoke Latin with a Punic accent.

b.                  b.    Severus’ second wife was a Syrian woman named Julia Domna, who bore him the sons Caracalla (Septimius Bassianus) and Geta (Septimius Geta).

c. 2)   Septimius avenged the murder of Pertinax, whose name he assumed, and disbanded the Italian Praetorian Guard. He replaced this with 15,000 of his best legionary soldiers from Illyria dn Thrace.

d.         3)      Septimius Severus acknowledged Clodius Albinus in Britain as his successor with the title Caesar. He did this to focus his armies against Pescennius Niger.

e. a.    Septimius Severus defeated Pescennius Niger and captured Antioch. Niger was killed aas he attmpted to escape to the Parthians across the Euphrates.

f.   b.    Byzantium fell to Severus in 195 AD and was virtually destroyed.

g. 4)   Septimius Severus fights Vologeses IV of Parthia: 194 to 195 AD

h.                  a.    Vologeses IV of Parthia had offered assistance to Niger and tampered with the loyalty of the king of Osrhoene, a Roman client in northwstern Mesopotamia.

i.   b.    Severus overran Osrhoene, northern Mesopotamia, and Adiabene, but then was recalled to fight against Clodius Ablinus.

j.    5)   Septimius Severus fights Clodius Albinus of Britain: 195 to 197 AD

k.                  a.    Clodius Albinus amassed an army in Britain because he was suspicious of Severus’ sincerity in acknowledging him Caesar and successor. 

l.   b.    In 195 AD, Severus had the army in Mesopotamia declare Albinus a public enemy.

m.               c.    Clodius Albinus set up headquarters at Lugdunum (Lyons). Severus collected his forces in Pannonia in 197 AD and marched on Gaul.

n.                  d.    At Lugdunum in 197 AD, Septimius Severus defeated Clodius Albinus. Severus allowed his soldiers to sack and burn Lugdunum. He purged Albinus’ supporters in the senate.

o.         6)      Severus was adopted into the famoly of Marcus Aurelius, the Antonines, and proclaimed the deification of his “brother” Commodus.

p.         7)      In 195 or 196 AD, Severus had the army proclaim his elder son Septimius Bassianus (later the emperor Caracalla) as Caesar in place of Clodius Albinus. Septimius Bassianus was called Marcus Aurelius Antoninus.

q.         8)      Septimius had his house worshipped as the domus divina in military camps. Septimius Severus was called more and more by the title dominus (Vespasian had been dominus et deus).

r.  a.    Septimius is numen praesens, Julia Domna is Cybele, Mater Senatus and Mater Patriae, Geta is Sol and “Son of Severus the Unconquered, Pius Augustus.”

s.  9)   Severus made the Italian senators a minority by appointed Africans and Eastern people.

t.   a.    Severus abolished the senatorial standing jury courts (quaestiones perpetuae) and placed their cases under the jurisdiction of the praefectus urbi and praectus praetorio.

u.         10)    Under Severus, the Praetorian Pefect became the commander-in-chief of all armed frces stationed in Italy, the man in charge of the grain supply, and vice president of the Imperial Council (now supreme court and legislative body of the Empire).

v. 11)       Trajan’s wife was Plotina. She helped to engineer the smooth succession of Hadrian.

w.      12)    197 to 205 AD: Septimius Severus’ senior prefect is C. Fulvius Plautianus

x. a.    Plautianus is Septimius Severus’ kinsman from Lepcis Magna.

y. b.    Plautianus virtually ruled the govenrmnet while Septimius played the part of the Imperial Warrior.

z. c.    Severus married his son Caracalla to Fulvia Plautilla, the daughter of Plautianus.

aa.    13)    Severus created a new treasury, the res privata principis, from confiscations, to augment the fiscus (the regular Imperial treasury) and the patrimonium Caesaris.

bb.            a.    Severus divided the Roman people into honestiores (more privileged honorable orders, including equites and all soldiers) and humiliores (less privileged humble people).

cc.              b.    Severus disbanded the Italian Praetorian Guard, stationed a newly cretaed legion in Italy, and appointed Near Eastern and African senators.  He legalized marraiges between soldiers and native women near the forts on the frontiers.

dd.            c.    Severus replaced senatorial legate commanders with equites in his reorganization of the army.

ee.             d.    Severus divided Britain and Syria each into two provinces, and separted Numidia from Africa.

ff.         14)    The Second Parthian War: 197 to 198 AD

gg.             a.    In imitation of Trajan, Severus went into Mesopotamia and sacked Ctesiphon, the Parthian capital.

hh.            b.    Severus recreated Trajan’s brief province of Mesopotamia.

ii.         15)    Syria, Arabia, Palestine and Egypt

jj. a.    Severus divided Syria into Syria Coele and Syria Phoenice (with its capital at Tyre), putting the rest in Arabia.

kk.            b.    In Egypt, Severus gratified the Jewish population, and closed the tomb of Alexander the Great, associated with Pescennius Niger. Pescennius Niger had claimed to be a second Alexander. Severus permitted Egyptians to become senators at Rome for the first time.  He was interested in Egyptian magic.

ll.         16)    Septimius Severus dedicated a triumphal arch in the Forum commemorating his victory in the First Parthian War. It towers over the Comitium, where the comitia curiata met.

mm.     17)       Caracalla hated his wife, Fulvia Plautilla, and her father, the prefect Plautianus. Caracalla, age 18 in 205 AD, had Plautianus assassinated and divorced Fulvia Plautilla. Caracalla then purged the senate of Plautianus’ supporters.

nn.            a.    Quintus Maecius Laetus and the distinguished jurist Papinian were appointed as joint Praetorian prefects.

oo.   18)    Septimius and Julia Domna, along with Caracalla and Geta, went to Britain to lead an expedition into Scotland.

pp.            a.    Septimius Severus rebuilt Hadrian’s Wall.

qq.            b.    Septimius Severus died at Eburacum (York) in 211 BC. He advised Caracalla and Geta on his deathbed to “agree with each other, enrich the soldiers, and despise everyone else.”

 

Caracalla (211 to 217 AD)

 

a. 1)   Caracalla and Geta ascended the throne together after Septimius Severus’ death.

b.         2)      Carcalla lured Geta to their mother Julia Domna’s appartment and murdred him in Domna’s very arms. Domna had tried to favor her more temperate son Geta, but then tried to make the best of it with Caracalla.

c. a.    Caracalla exterminated Geta’s supporters, including Papinian.

d.                  b.    Caracalla issued a new coin, the Antonianus, supposedly twice a denarius.

e. 3)   The name Caracalla came from a long Gallic cape he wore.

f.   4)   Caracalla’s new Praetorian Prefect was Marcus Opellius Macrinus.

g. 5)   Caracalla promulgated the Constitutio Antoniniana, the Antonine Constitution, in 212 AD, which extended citizenship to all free inhabitants of the Empire.

h.         6)      Caracalla’s German Wars

i.   a.    In 213 Caracalla went to the border of Raetia to attack the Geramn Alemmani.

j.    b.    Caracalla defeated the Alemmani at the river Main.

k.         7)      Carcalla’s Parthian Wars against Artabanus V of Parthia

l.   a.    Caracalla proposed to marry the daughter of Artabanus V of Parthia. Artabanus rejected and Cracalla went to fight him.

m.               b.    Caracalla marched across Adiabene and invaded Media, but then retired to his winter quarters at Edessa (Urfa) in Osrheone.

n.                  c.    Caracalla was stabbed at the instigation of his Praetorian Prefect Macrinus, as he was travelling from Edessa to Carrhae to worship at the Temple of the Moon.

 

Macrinus (217 to 218 AD)

 

a. 1)   Macrinus was ringleader of the plot against Caracalla, and a Mauretanian eques.

b.         2)      Macrinus was the first princeps without prior experience in the Senate to become emperor.

c. 3)   Macrinus adopted the name of Severus and bestowed the name Antoninus upon his young son Diadumneianus. Macrinus ordered the senate to proclaim Caracalla a god.

d.         4)      Macrinus continued Caracalla’s war against Artabanus V of Parthia. But eventually he lost his own troops’ support through an unfavorable agreement with Artabanus V, and was deserted by his own troops in favor of Elagabalus.

 

Elagabalus (218 to 212 AD)

 

a. 1)   First, some info about some Syrian Queens.

b.                  a.    Julia Domna was the wife of Septimius Severus. Julia Domna’s sister was Julia Maesa, and Julia Maesa’s daughters were Julia Soaemias and Julia Mamaea.

c. b.    Julia Domna and Julia Maesa were daughters of the high priest of the sun god Elagablus in the Arabian city of Emesa in Syria.

i.                 i.     Plautianus had superseded Julia Domna in influence with Severus.

d.                  c.    Julia Domna accompanied Caracalla to Parthia for his war against Artabanus V of Parthia, but died there soon after Caracalla was assasinated.

e. d.    Macrinus forced Julia Maesa, sister of Julia Domna, to retire to Syria.

f.   e.    In Syria, Julia Maesa plotted with her daughters Julia Mamaea and Julia Soaemias. Julia Soaemias had a son: Elagabalus (so named because he had inherited the priesthood of Elagabalus; his real name was Varius Avitus). Julia Mamaea also had a son, Gessius Bassianus Alexianus, later the emperor Severus Alexander

g. f.     Julia Maesa concocted the rumor that her grandson Elagabalus was the real son of Cracalla, and therefore a real Severus. The legions of Syria saluted Elagablus (Varius Avitus) as emperor under the name Marcus Aurelius Antoninus, Caracalla’s royal name.

h.         2)      Elagabalus arrived at Rome wearing a purple silk robe, rouge on his cheeks, a necklace of pearls, and a bejeweled crown. He imported a conical black stone, the cult image of the god Elagabalus, to Rome from Emesa.

i.   a.    Elagabalus enshrined the stone in a temple on the Palatine and worshipped it with sex, drums, cymbals, and singing of Syrian women.

j.    b.    Elagabalus made the sun good Elagabalus the supreme diety of the Roman state.

k.         3)      Elagabalus entrusted most of the duties of state to his mother Julia Maesa. But Maesa saw that Elagabalus would lead to the downfall of the Severans and suggested that Elagabalus adopt his cousin, Julia Mamaea’s son Severus Alexander (then Gessius Bassianus Alexianus), under the name Marcus Aurelius Severus Alexander.

l.   a.    Severus Alexander was preferred by the senate and people; Elagabalus regretted his decision and twice tried to kill Severus Alexander.

m.      4)      Julia Mamaea and her mother Julia Maesa appealed to the Praetorian Guard, who hunted down and killed Elagabalus and his mother Julia Soaemias as they hid in a latrine. The Praetorians dragged Soaemias and Elagabalus’ corpses through the streets of Rome to the Aemilian bridge (pons Aemilianus), tying them down with weights and hurling them into the Tiber.

 

Severus Alexander (222 to 235 AD)

 

a. 1)   Several Alexander’s real name was Gessius Bassianus Alexianus, as his cousin Elagabalus’ real name was Varius Avitus.

b.         2)      Severus Alexander was only fourteen, and his domineering mother Julia Mamaea really held power.

c. 3)   Severus Alexander instituted primary school ecuation all across the Empire, legalized guilds (collegia), and reduced taxes.

d.         4)      Severus Alexander’s praetorian prefect was Ulpian (Domitius Ulpianus), a distinguished jurist.

e. a.    In 228, the Praetorian Guard mutinied and murdered its prefect Ulpian, because he was too strict.

i.                 i.     The historian Cassius Dio would have been killed two by mutiny if Severus Alexander hadn’t sent him back to Bithynia, his homeland.

ii.         5)      In Parthia, the Arsacid regime was overthrown by the Sassanid dynasty of Ardashir (Artaxerxes I) and his son Shapur I.

f.   a.    Artaxerxes I and Shapur I tried to reesstablish the old Persian Empire of the Achaemenids.

g. b.    Severus Alexander went to the East and fought against Artaxerxes I. He didn’t manage to make much progress but celebrated a triumph in Rome.

h.         6)      The Alemannia and other German tribes were breaking into Gaul and Raetia. Severus Alexander bought them off, and his men were pissed off, because they wanted the money for themselves, so Maximinus Thrax rebelled.

i.   7)   Maximinus Thrax (Gaius Iulius Verus Maximus), a Thracian and commander of the Pannonian legions, mutinied and killed Severus Alexander and his mother Julia Mamaea. With Severus Alexanders’ death, the Severan dynasty ended.
