Numa Pompilius (717 BC – 673 BC)


Numa Pompilius was a Sabine man born at Cures. Numa Pompilius’ father was named Pomponius.

Numa Pompilius was married to Tatia, the daughter of Titus Tatius.

Numa Pompilius’ daughter Pompilia was the mother of Ancus Marcius.

Numa Pompilius built the Temple of Janus. When the doors of the Temple of Janus are closed, this signifies that the Romans are at peace.

Numa Pompilius divided the year into 12 lunar months, fixing the calendar.

Numa Pompilius established the priestly colleges along with the flamines, including the flamen dialis (priest of Jupiter), the flamen martialis (priest of Mars) and flamen Quirinalis (priest of Quirinus, the deified Romulus).

Numa Pompilius also imported the cult of Vesta from Alba Longa.

Numa Pompilius introduced the 12 Salii, jumping/dancing priests of Mars Gradivus who carried ancilia (sacred shields).

Numa Pompilius consecrated a temple to Jupiter Elicius on the Aventine.

Numa Pompilius instituted an annual ceremony dedicated to trothkeeping.

Numa Pompilius fixed the lawful and unlawful days (fas and nefas) for conducting business.

Numa Pompilius appointed Numa Marcius as pontifex maximus.

Numa Pompilius visited the nymph Egeria to get advice.
