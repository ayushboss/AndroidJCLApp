Tullus Hostilius (673 – 641 BC)


Tullus Hostilius was of Roman ethnicity, and born at Rome. Tullus Hostilius was the grandson of Hostius Hostilius, who died fighting against the Sabines during the reign of Romulus.

Tullus Hostilius was, similarly to Romulus, raised by shepherds.

Tullus Hostilius built the Curia Hostilia, Rome’s first senate house.

Tullus Hostilius built his palace on the Caelian Hill.

Tullus Hostilius defeated the Sabines at Mantrap Wood.

Tullus Hostilius Fights Alba Longa (Part I)

The war against Alba Longa started with a series of reciprocal cattle raids.

Cluilius was king of Alba Longa, succeeded by the commander Mettius Fufettius, whom Tullus Hostilius eventually had drawn and quartered.

The Roman Horatii triplets fought the Alban Curiatii triplets. One Horatius, called Publius Horatius, remained after the battle. When his sister Horatia (who had been engaged to a Curiatius) wept, Horatius killed her.

The Romans and Albans concluded a treaty.

Tullus Hostilius Fights Alba Longa (Part II)

Mettius Fufettius and the Alba Longans were supposed to help Rome in its fight against Fidenae and Veii, but Fufettius treacherously waited to see who would win.

Tullus Hostilius had Mettius Fufettius drawn and quartered for his treachery.

Rome sacked and destroyed Alba Longa, transferring its population to Rome and thereby doubling the Roman population.

Tullus Hostilius died after being struck by a thunderbolt, due to performing the rites of Jupiter Elicius improperly.
